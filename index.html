Climate Vulnerability in India

India is the seventh most vulnerable country with regards to climate extremes (Germanwatch2020, cited in Mohanty & Wadhawan, n.d.).

















Climate Vulnerability Index (CVI)

Climate Vulnerability Index, or CVI, is a micro-level vulnerability assessment that maps the climate vulnerability for the states in India. This is based on the IPCC’s SREX framework, and takes exposure, sensitivity, and adaptive capacity, the three key elements of vulnerability into consideration. Exposure is the likelihood of hazards to occur due to the climate and physical geography as well as the hazard’s intensity and duration. Sensitivity relates to the hazard’s impact on people and livelihoods, and Adaptive Capacity is the ability for the location or region to cope from the impacts of the hazard. Social and Economic factors play a large role in Sensitivity and Adaptive Capacity.
